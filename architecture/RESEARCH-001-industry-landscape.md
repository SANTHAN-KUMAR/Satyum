# RESEARCH-001 — Industry Landscape: How Banks & Governments Actually Verify Documents

> Cited web research (2024–2026) grounding [ADR-001](ADR-001-dual-mode-and-signal-validity.md).
> Generated 2026-06-27 via a 5-angle web-search → adversarial fact-check → synthesis workflow.
> Confidence and caveats are in §6 — read them; several adoption stats are vendor-sourced.


## 1. Bottom Line

Industry reality broadly **validates** Satyum's dual-mode posture, but with one decisive reframing. For every document class examined — financial statements, identity documents, and land/title records — the institutional best practice is **verification against an authoritative source** (open-banking/Account-Aggregator pull, government-signed chips/registries, title-plant databases), not pixel-level analysis of an uploaded image. So "provenance/source default" is correct as the primary path. The single most important correction is this: **Satyum's "file/forensic" mode is not a legacy fallback — it is a load-bearing, permanent second tier**, because source connectivity has real coverage gaps (banks not on aggregators, thin-file/self-employed borrowers, foreign and long-tail documents, scanned paper deeds). The second-most-important correction is that **"camera escalation" is itself under active attack**: digital injection attacks now bypass the camera entirely, so simply "turning on a live camera" is not a strengthening of assurance unless paired with injection-resistant capture and, for identity, NFC chip cryptography. Finally, Satyum should narrow its forensic ambitions sharply: classical pixel forensics (ELA, PRNU, copy-move) is genuinely distrusted by practitioners, but **document-structure/metadata forensics and OCR arithmetic cross-checks remain trusted and recommended** — these are different things and should not be lumped together. Net: confirm the direction, reposition the file path as durable, and harden the camera path.

## 3. Camera vs. Provenance vs. Forensics: The Verdict

**Where live camera capture genuinely fits.** Camera + liveness is the production norm for **identity onboarding** at the major IDV vendors (Onfido/Entrust, Jumio, Veriff) and is the biometric anchor that binds a person to a document. It is the right control for the **person** behind a transaction — including the seller/owner in deed-fraud scenarios (RON, HMLR Safe Harbour). It is **not** the right primary control for financial statements (irrelevant) or for the deed/instrument itself (a registry problem). Caveat (flagged as OVERCLAIMED in verification): "live capture has replaced uploaded stills" is too strong — vendors still support photo-upload fallbacks for lower-assurance tiers; live capture is the high-assurance default, not a universal replacement.

**Where cryptographic provenance / source connectivity dominates.** This is the verification-of-record across all three classes where it is available:
- Financial: Fannie Mae DU/D1C, The Work Number (~149M verifications in 2024, ~400k/day), UK AISP open banking, India AA (~120M linked accounts by Dec 2024, >USD 10B disbursed). Source validation is what confers legal/regulatory standing.
- Identity: NFC chip Passive Authentication against the ICAO PKD; PAdES/QES (eIDAS Art. 25 = handwritten-signature equivalence).
- Land: title-plant databases, India DILRMP/NGDRS, PAdES-signed born-digital deeds.

But provenance is **a strong signal, not absolute proof** (well-supported caveat): C2PA has live demonstrated exploits (forged-but-valid manifests via `c2patool`; the Sept 2025 Nikon Z6 III multiple-exposure exploit that signed an AI-generated image with a Trust-List-backed signature, after which Nikon suspended C2PA and revoked all certs). C2PA is opt-in, strippable, and proves a device signed a file — not that the device captured the depicted scene ("first-mile" problem). RAND (Jun 2025) explicitly warns against overpromising on provenance. Likewise NFC Passive Authentication **does not detect a cloned chip** — only optional Active/Chip Authentication (EAC) does, and these are not mandatory for issuing countries.

**Real-world standing of classical pixel forensics.** Distrusted, and the evidence is strong. ELA is described by named experts as performing at roughly chance (Hany Farid) and "subjective... a method used by hobbyists" (Jens Kriese); even its popularizer concedes interpretation rests entirely on the viewer. PRNU is structurally unsuited to underwriting uploads (needs a reference set from the exact camera; degraded by compression/scaling; splice-only). ML tamper detectors collapse out-of-distribution — TruFor falls from AUC 0.96 on NIST16 to **0.751 zero-shot** on AI-generated document forgeries (AIForge-Doc), exactly the 2025–2026 threat.

**Critical distinction Satyum must not blur:** practitioners draw a sharp line between distrusted *pixel* forensics (ELA/PRNU/copy-move) and **trusted *structure/metadata* forensics** — PDF Producer/Creator field (e.g., "Microsoft Word"/"Canva"/"Photoshop" in a bank-statement producer field is a strong forgery signal), modification-date anomalies, font/decimal-column alignment, template fingerprinting against known bank templates, and **OCR + running-balance arithmetic** (previous balance ± debit/credit = new balance). These remain high-signal, actively recommended, and robust to recompression. "Image forensics is weak" must not be overgeneralized to "all file-level forensics is weak."

## 4. The 2024–2026 Threat Shift

The decisive change is the rise of **digital injection attacks** — feeding pre-recorded or fully synthetic video directly into the verification data stream, bypassing the physical camera. This is distinct from (and more scalable than) presentation attacks shown to a camera, because it needs no physical artifact. iProov reported a 2,665% spike in native virtual-camera attacks and a 300% jump in face-swaps vs. 2023, tracking 127 face-swap tools / 91 virtual cameras / 10 emulators (~115,000 attack combinations); injection attacks rose 783% in 2024, with a later report citing a 741% annual increase and a 1,151% surge on iOS in H2 2025. Jumio reported an 88% YoY rise; Sumsub puts deepfakes at ~11% of fraud schemes in 2026 (up from 7% in 2024). The canonical materiality case is **Arup Hong Kong** (Jan 2024): HK$200M (~US$25.6M) wired after a deepfake video call.

Critically, **ISO/IEC 30107-3 PAD certification explicitly excludes injection attacks** — so a vendor's iBeta Level 2/3 liveness conformance is necessary but **not sufficient**. Regulators have responded: NIST SP 800-63A-4 makes virtual-camera/emulator/jailbreak detection and live capture a **SHALL**, and ISO 30107-3:2023 PAD conformance a SHALL (IAPAR < 0.07). (Calibration note from verification: analyzing media for generative-AI/deepfake signatures is a **SHOULD**, not a SHALL — the earlier claim that NIST *requires* GenAI media analysis is OVERCLAIMED.) EU eIDAS 2.0 / ETSI TS 119 461 v2 (Feb 2025) similarly codify liveness and injection defenses.

**Implication for Satyum's camera-escalation mode:** a camera escalation that only inspects the final image gives false confidence — "defenses that only inspect the final image cannot see" an injected stream. The genuine escalation is (a) **capture/data-stream integrity** (proving the feed came from a genuine camera sensor — Veriff/Regula's design emphasis, corroborated by NIST's "genuine sensor" control) plus virtual-camera/emulator detection, and (b) for identity documents, **NFC chip read** as the cryptographic anchor that does not depend on the camera feed at all. "Camera escalation" should be renamed and re-scoped to "injection-resistant capture + chip read," not "turn on the webcam."

## 5. What Satyum Is Missing vs. Best Practice (Prioritized)

A document-integrity console that only checks file provenance/authenticity does not, by itself, address the dominant institutional risks (defective chain of title, account takeover, synthetic identity, seller impersonation). Concrete gaps and repositioning, in priority order:

1. **Source connectivity as a first-class verification-of-record path (highest priority, financial).** Integrate open-banking / Account-Aggregator pull (Plaid/Finicity/MX in US/UK; RBI AA / FIP-signed FI in India). Treat a successful source pull as conferring the equivalent of rep-and-warranty relief, and treat a PDF-only submission where a pull was possible as a **red flag**. This removes the tampering attack surface rather than trying to detect attacks on it.

2. **Reposition the file/forensic path as a permanent, calibrated fallback — not a default to be deprecated.** Coverage gaps (cooperative banks, RRBs, small finance banks not AA-enabled; self-employed/thin-file; foreign/long-tail documents; scanned paper deeds) make it load-bearing. Calibrate explicitly for false positives from legitimate print-to-PDF, mobile-scan, compression, and file-merge workflows ("metadata alone is no longer enough").

3. **Injection-attack defense for the camera mode (highest priority, identity).** Add data-stream/sensor-integrity verification, virtual-camera/emulator/jailbreak detection, and synthetic-media detection. Without this, the camera escalation is itself the weakest link. Target ISO 30107-3:2023 PAD conformance *and* go beyond it (since PAD excludes injection).

4. **NFC ePassport chip reading + Passive Authentication for identity escalation.** This is the stronger escalation than a live camera — government-signed source data ranked above optical forgery detection. Flag the clone limitation (Passive Authentication ≠ anti-clone; AA/EAC optional and unevenly issued).

5. **Cryptographic provenance verification (C2PA / PAdES / eIDAS QES) for born-digital documents — treated as a strong signal, not proof.** Verify PAdES/QES signature chains on signed PDFs (free EC DSS validator exists). Validate C2PA manifests where present, but design for opt-in absence-of-signal, strip attacks, and the first-mile gap; never render "signed = authentic" as dispositive.

6. **Land-registry cross-reference and the legal/insurance dimension (land).** A pure file-provenance console does not address title verification at all. Integrate registry source-connectivity (DILRMP/ULPIN/NGDRS in India; title-plant data in US) and recognize that the binding controls are an advocate's title opinion / title insurance — risk-transfer and legal processes Satyum cannot replace, only feed. Cover EC blind spots (unregistered settlements, pending litigation) via eCourts.

7. **Cross-institution / consortium network signals (financial).** Add network fraud-signal participation (Plaid Beacon-style) for identity-fraud, ATO, and synthetic-identity risks that no document inspection can reveal.

8. **OCR arithmetic + structure/metadata checks as the durable forensic core; down-weight pixel forensics.** Make running-balance arithmetic, cross-field consistency, PDF Producer/Creator anomalies, and template fingerprinting the backbone of the file path. Avoid building an ML-heavy pixel-detection layer that will give false confidence against the exact GenAI/diffusion forgeries now emerging.

## 6. Honest Confidence and Caveats

**Well-supported (high confidence, multiple corroborating or primary sources):**
- Source connectivity is the verification-of-record for financial data and confers rep-and-warranty relief (Fannie Mae selling guide, D1C, The Work Number figures, UK FCA AISP, India AA/CGAP/Sahamati). SUPPORTED across the board.
- Live capture + liveness is the high-assurance identity norm; PAD is formalized via ISO 30107-3 / iBeta. SUPPORTED.
- Injection attacks are the dominant 2024–2026 identity threat and bypass the camera; Arup case verified. SUPPORTED.
- NFC chip Passive Authentication is higher-assurance than optical, but does not detect clones (AA/EAC optional). SUPPORTED.
- ELA/PRNU pixel forensics distrusted; ML detectors collapse on AI-generated forgeries (TruFor 0.96→0.751). SUPPORTED.
- Title verification is registry/database + legal opinion + insurance, not image forensics; UK HMLR Safe Harbour mandates NFC + rejects ID photos. SUPPORTED.
- C2PA/provenance is not a silver bullet (Nikon Z6 III, c2patool exploits, RAND warning). SUPPORTED — best-calibrated claim in the set.

**Down-weighted / flagged as OVERCLAIMED (use with caution):**
- "Removing the upload step makes document fraud *disappear entirely*" — directionally right but over-strong; source-pull removes *one* vector (file tampering) and shifts risk to ATO/synthetic identity/connection-spoofing (which is why Beacon exists).
- "Live capture has *replaced* uploaded stills as the universal norm" — overstated; upload fallbacks persist for lower-assurance tiers; sourcing is partly vendor marketing.
- "NIST *requires* generative-AI media analysis" — it is a SHOULD, not a SHALL; virtual-camera/emulator detection and live capture are the SHALLs.
- "ELA is useless against GenAI" — the expert critique of ELA is supported, but the specific GenAI extension was a reasonable inference not backed by the cited source.

**Thin / unverified specifics (do not treat as load-bearing):**
- Several adoption stats ride on vendor blogs rather than neutral regulators: the "single most effective measure" framing (Precisa/proanalyser), CKYC sector-split and onboarding-speed numbers (Surepass/Shuftipro), and the "~1 in 5 applicants misrepresents income" figure could not be pinned to a primary source.
- Attribution slippage in GenAI-fraud stats: the 311% figure is Sumsub (not Sift); the 456% is Sift; Deloitte's $40B (2027) is the aggressive scenario (~$22B conservative).
- Minor numeric drift, non-load-bearing: The Work Number contributing-employer count (~4.2–4.6M, not "5M+"); "1/3 of title searches reveal a problem" (industry stat is closer to ~1 in 4); India title-search depth (13–15 vs 30 years varies by lender/state); the "Sage Home Loans" open-banking example unconfirmed.
- Maturity is uneven and must not be assumed universal: India's source-pull rails (DigiLocker, AA, Aadhaar eSign, CKYC) are in mass production now, but the EU's document-provenance equivalent (EUDI Wallet / QEAA / PuB-EAA) is pre-production, mandated only by end-2026 with several member states signaling delays — so for EU-facing underwriting, PAdES/QES is the "today" provenance layer. India NGDRS blockchain is POC, not deployed. Blockchain land registries (Georgia–Hedera MoU is exploratory; Sweden's €100M/yr is projected, not realized) are real but not productionized at the scale sometimes implied.

**One detail correction in the source data:** the claim that India eSign ESPs are "only NSDL and CDAC" is outdated — CCA empanels ~18 eSign Service Providers (eMudhra, Protean/NSDL, CDAC, Capricorn, etc.). The surrounding legal-equivalence thesis is sound.
